First, the principle. A beachhead comes from military strategy: land your limited forces on one narrow beach, secure it completely, then advance. In startup terms: choose one segment, dominate it, and use it as your launchpad. And the counterintuitive part is that the beachhead should be small. You want to be a big fish in a small pond: visible, referenceable, dominant. Not plankton in an ocean. The continent's best climate ventures all show this discipline. Ampersand did not target African transport; it targeted moto-taxi riders in one city. BasiGo did not target electric vehicles for Africa; it targeted the operators already running Nairobi's bus routes. Easy Solar picked Sierra Leone, a market the giants ignored, and became its reference brand before expanding to Liberia. Winning a small pond completely beats owning a fraction of a percent of a huge one, because ponds you dominate produce the references, learning and cash that buy your ticket to the next pond.
