Now the stress test: Bill Aulet's seven questions, plus two of our own. One: is the segment well-funded? And note what this means in our markets. Well-funded does not mean wealthy. It means the money already flows. Seventeen billion dollars a year already goes to kerosene. A rider already buys three litres of petrol daily. A trader already fills crates every morning. If the status quo captures your customer's money, the budget exists; your job is to redirect it. Two: can you actually reach these customers with the team and channels you have? Three: do they have a compelling reason to buy now, a pain felt today? Four: can you deliver a complete product, a whole solution, not a component they must assemble? Five: who is the entrenched competition, and remember from last module that this includes the status quo. Six: can you leverage this segment to enter others; is it a springboard or a dead end? Seven: is this market consistent with your passion, because you will live in it for years. Then add the sustainability pair. Eight: are there social issues here you can help solve? Nine: can you reduce the environmental footprint? Score every candidate segment on all nine, with evidence, not gut feel. This exercise does not just rank your options. Very often, it changes them.
