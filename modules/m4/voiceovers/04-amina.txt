A segment is an abstraction, and nobody sells to an abstraction. So turn your beachhead into a persona. Meet Amina. She is thirty-eight, farms one and a half acres of vegetables near Machakos in Kenya, and her household earns around one hundred and fifty dollars a month, spiking at harvest. She finished primary school and has farmed for fifteen years. What motivates her? Her children's school fees, and being seen as a serious commercial farmer. What does she fear? A failed season, and debt. Now the two blocks founders always skip. Proxy products: what does she already buy? Hybrid seed and fertiliser each season, eight dollars of airtime a month, a weekly contribution to her savings group, all paid through M-Pesa. That is your best evidence of willingness to pay, and of the shape her payments must take. And watering holes: where does she get information? Vernacular radio in the morning, a WhatsApp group of vegetable farmers, her agro-dealer's shop, the cooperative's monthly meeting. That is where your marketing will live, not on a billboard. Build your persona from interview evidence, give her a name, and from now on, let your whole team ask one question in every product debate: would Amina buy this?
