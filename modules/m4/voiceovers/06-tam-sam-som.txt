Now, the numbers. Learn three nested figures. TAM, the total addressable market: everyone you could ever serve, the theoretical maximum. SAM, the serviceable addressable market: the part you could reach in the medium term, given your focus and location. SOM, the serviceable obtainable market: what you can win now with your current resources. And in the very centre, your earlyvangelists: the customers you can actually name. There are two ways to calculate. Top-down starts from a big published number and slices it. It is fast, and dangerously flattering; every "if we win just one percent" plan in history was built this way. Use it only as a sanity check. Bottom-up is what investors believe, because it starts from unit economics. Try it for an e-moto venture. A rider spends about two dollars fifty a day on swap energy: call it nine hundred dollars a year. Four hundred riders at the stages you already serve: three hundred and sixty thousand dollars a year. Your next funding round finances ten more stations reaching three thousand riders: about two point seven million. Kigali plus two Kenyan cities: twenty-seven million. The continent's three million moto-taxis: a multi-billion-dollar energy market. Every step is visible, every assumption is written down, and the near numbers are credible because they are built from real riders at real stages.
