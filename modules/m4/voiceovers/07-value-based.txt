But what if your market does not exist yet in any report? Then use the value-based approach: estimate what customers would pay for the improvement you offer, and multiply by how many of them there are. The classic global example is Spotify, which priced access to all music at ten dollars a month and multiplied by the world's music listeners, at a time when the download statistics said streaming was nothing. But the strongest example is African. In twenty eleven, the solar home system market in East Africa was officially tiny; a top-down analysis would have said: no market. The value-based question was different. What do off-grid households already pay for bad energy: kerosene, candles, phone-charging kiosks? Around seventeen billion dollars a year, in exactly the small daily amounts a pay-as-you-go plan could capture. That reframing revealed one of Africa's largest consumer markets years before it appeared in any industry report. M-Kopa, Sun King and d-light went on to build it, and Sun King alone now serves around fifty million people. Value-based sizing tells you two things at once: how big the prize is, and what your product must cost per day to claim it.
