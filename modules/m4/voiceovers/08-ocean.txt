One more scan before you commit: the Ocean of Opportunities. Draw a two-by-two grid. Choose two dimensions that describe solutions in your market: say, fossil versus electric on one axis, and owned versus pay-per-use on the other. Now plot every real solution you can find, including the informal ones and the status quo. Petrol motorcycles, owned. Matatus. Fuel stations. Then the electric column: e-motos with swap networks, electric buses on battery leases. And now look at the empty cells. Electric rural transport. E-cargo services in secondary cities. Empty cells are candidate blue oceans: spaces with little or no competition. A few rules. Use qualitative endpoints, not numbers. Avoid judgement words like better or worse. Test your axes by placing real businesses in the corners. And iterate with different axis pairs, because every new pair reveals new gaps. This canvas returns in the next module as a competition tool.
