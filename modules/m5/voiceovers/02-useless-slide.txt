You know the two classics. Version one: the feature table, ten rows, five competitor columns, and by some miracle only your startup ticks every box. Version two: the two-axis chart where, by an equal miracle, only your startup sits in the top right corner. Investors have seen thousands of these. They know the game, so they skip the slide, and at best they harvest the competitor names for their own research. Why do these slides fail? First, they all look identical, whether the startup makes solar pumps, insurance or recycled bricks, and a slide that cannot distinguish you carries no information. Second, they are built on features, but customers do not buy features. They buy solutions to felt problems, and every buying decision, even in B2B, comes down in the end to people and their feelings. And third, most dangerously, these slides hide your three most important rivals entirely.
