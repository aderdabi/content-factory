So here is the method, in three steps. Step one: identify. List your direct competitors, both flavours of indirect, and the potential entrants who have the capability and a motive to enter. Write a plain description of the status quo: what does your customer do today, and what does it cost them? And list the plausible industry shifts: regulation, technology, currency, climate. Step two: analyse each serious player on eight criteria. Capabilities: what does their product actually do? USP: what is genuinely special, including beyond the product: support, financing, brand, convenience? Target markets. Sales model: agents, retail, partners, tenders, digital? Partnerships, and remember the investor proverb: distribution beats product. First-time founders worry about product; second-time founders worry about distribution. Possible extensions: what could they launch next? Size. And funding: how much, and from whom, because the identity of the investors tells you the strategy and the staying power. A rival backed by a patient development bank plays a completely different game from one backed by a growth fund that needs a result in three years.
