Let me show you what a mature competitive landscape looks like, using pay-as-you-go solar: one market, five completely different games. Sun King is the volume leader: roughly thirty-eight percent of the global market, more than thirty million products sold, an army of door-to-door agents, one relentless vertical. D-light has touched a similar number of lives, but its sharpest edge is financial engineering: over a billion dollars of receivables securitised, which means it recycles capital faster than anyone. M-Kopa followed its credit rails out of solar entirely, into smartphones, loans and insurance; today it competes as much with banks as with solar firms. Bboxx positions as a next-generation utility: one platform managing solar, cooking gas, water pumps and e-mobility, with strong government partnerships. And Easy Solar wins by geography: dominant in Sierra Leone and Liberia, the frontier markets the giants left alone. Here is the point. No feature table separates these five; they differ by value proposition, segment, geography and financing model, exactly the lenses this course teaches. And notice one more thing: all five still spend most of their energy fighting the same incumbent. Kerosene. The status quo remains the biggest player on the map.
