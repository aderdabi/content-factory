Step three: map everyone on two axes. Market overlap: do they chase your customers? And solution overlap: is their offer like yours? Four quadrants, four strategies. High market overlap, low solution overlap: partner. These players serve your customers with complementary offers; they are allies, channels, sometimes your eventual acquirer. High and high: direct threat. Differentiate on your value proposition where they are weak. Low and low: observe, with a calendar reminder. Low market overlap but high solution overlap: risk. These are the indirect rivals with the technology and money to enter your market; build your barriers now, through deep customer relationships and switching costs. And add one more dimension: study how each rival competes, not just where. In African e-mobility, Ampersand grows from unit economics: prove the swap beats petrol for one rider, one stage, one city, then repeat, and it has now opened its network to other manufacturers, aiming to become the neutral energy layer. Spiro, backed by more than three hundred and thirty million dollars, blitzscales through government agreements and aggressive pricing across six countries at once. Same market, same solution, opposite games. Your response to each must be different, and your map should say so.
