One last lens: the colour of your ocean. Crowded cells on your opportunity map are red oceans: you will bleed on price there. Empty cells are blue oceans: little competition, no feature war. Ask honestly which one you are swimming in, and whether a repositioning, a different segment, a different business model, could move you toward open water. And for climate ventures, remember that regulation moves the tide in both directions. Plastic-bag bans in Rwanda and Kenya created instant blue oceans for alternatives. E-mobility incentives and fuel-import pressure are opening the electric ocean right now. Easy Solar found a blue ocean simply by serving countries others ignored. But the tide also goes out: one denied carbon authorisation closed KOKO's ocean overnight. Watch the regulatory tide the way a sailor watches weather.
