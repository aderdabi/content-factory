Start with the anatomy. Every business model has four building blocks. The value proposition: what value do you offer? Value creation: how do you produce it? Value delivery: how does it reach the customer? And value capture: how does money flow back? A sustainable business model upgrades all four: measurable impact in the proposition, responsible suppliers in creation, customers nudged toward responsible consumption in delivery, and fair distribution of costs and benefits in capture. Then judge any model, yours or a competitor's, on three levers. Production cost: keep it low, and notice that resource efficiency means sustainability and margin often point the same way. Customer acquisition cost: keep it low, through short sales cycles and simple processes. And customer lifetime value: keep it high, through strong pricing, retention and recurring revenue. The golden rule: lifetime value must comfortably exceed acquisition cost plus production cost. And know your model's shape in time. M-Kopa needed thirteen years to reach its first profit, because pay-as-you-go models spend money on hardware and agents today and collect value in fifty-cent instalments over years. There is nothing wrong with that shape, but you must see it clearly and finance it deliberately. A brilliant model you cannot finance is a beautiful way to die.
