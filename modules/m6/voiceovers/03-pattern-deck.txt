Now the liberating news: you do not have to invent your business model. The patterns already exist, and Africa runs them all. Straight product sale: Gjenge Makers selling pavers, Kubik selling walls cheaper than cement. Pay-per-use: ColdHubs charging per crate per day, Ampersand charging per swap. Asset financing, the continent's flagship pattern, born of mobile money: a small deposit, tiny instalments, ownership at the end, remote lockout keeping the credit honest. M-Kopa for solar and phones, SunCulture's pay-as-you-grow for irrigation. Third-party-pays: the user pays little or nothing, and the party who captures value pays instead; hold that thought for the Pula story in a few minutes. Razor and blade: KOKO selling a twelve-dollar stove to sell fuel for years. Subscription. Two-sided marketplace: Hello Tractor connecting three thousand tractor owners with half a million farmers, so the owner monetises idle machine time and the farmer ploughs forty times faster than by hand. And performance models: BasiGo selling kilometres of reliable bus operation rather than batteries. Beyond these, the Business Model Navigator catalogues fifty-five patterns. Treat them as a deck of cards to deal against your venture. And remember: winners combine. M-Kopa stacks asset financing with lock-in, credit data and agent distribution. Sanergy runs a franchise pattern feeding a trash-to-cash pattern.
