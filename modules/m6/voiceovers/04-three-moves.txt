How do you design with patterns? Three moves. Move one, similarity: map your ecosystem first. What is the dominating business model in your sector? What do customers, partners and competitors need? Which change drivers, technology, megatrends, regulation, are shaking the ground? That tells you where the room for innovation is. Move two, confrontation: force patterns that are foreign to your market onto it, because that is where disruption comes from. Nobody sold electricity as a daily instalment until pay-as-you-go transferred consumer-credit logic to energy. Nobody sold bus batteries by the kilometre until BasiGo transferred aviation's power-by-the-hour engine leasing to Nairobi transit. Deal the fifty-five cards one by one and ask of each: would this cut entry barriers? Create recurring revenue? Scale? Enable circularity? A playful shortcut: ask what would Apple do with your product. What would Safaricom do? What would IKEA do? Each question reliably generates a different family of models. Move three, testing: check internal consistency, that your who, what, how and why reinforce each other, and external consistency, that the model fits the context map from Module two. Then stop theorising. Take the model to pilot customers, present it, listen hard, adapt. Test, learn, adapt, test. Forever.
