Now, the circular families, because circularity is not charity; it is a set of proven revenue logics. Picture the Value Hill. On the way up, value is created: raw material becomes component becomes product. At the summit, the use phase, value is preserved. And on the way down, value is recaptured: reuse, repair, remanufacture, recycle. In a linear economy there is no downhill path; the product simply falls off a cliff into the dumpsite, and its value crashes to zero. Circular businesses are simply companies positioned on the summit and the downhill slope. The nine-R ladder ranks the strategies by how much value they retain. At the top: refuse, rethink, reduce. Hello Tractor rethinks ownership itself: why should every farmer own a tractor that sits idle? A swap network reduces: one battery serves many riders. In the middle: reuse, repair, refurbish, remanufacture, repurpose. Kubik and Gjenge repurpose plastic waste into buildings. At the bottom, as last resorts, recycle and recover: Sanergy recovering nutrients from sanitation waste, Mr. Green Africa recycling plastics into traceable resin that global brands buy at a premium. Every rung of the ladder has an African business standing on it, earning.
