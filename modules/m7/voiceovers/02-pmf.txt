Phase one targets product-market fit. Your value hypothesis is the key assumption about why a customer will use your product; product-market fit is the state where the market has proven it. How does it feel? Without fit, everything is heavy: word of mouth is silent, usage is flat, sales cycles drag, deals stall politely forever. With fit, everything breaks in the good direction: customers buy as fast as you can deliver, and demand outruns supply. In hardware, fit looks like BasiGo's five hundred paid deposits waiting for buses. But guard against four myths, courtesy of investor Ben Horowitz. Myth one: fit is a single big-bang event; in reality it arrives gradually and unevenly. Myth two: it is obvious when you have it; often it is not. Myth three: once achieved, it cannot be lost; markets shift, rivals improve, regulations change, and fit evaporates. Myth four: with fit, you can ignore competition; you cannot. Product-market fit is not a trophy on a shelf. It is a state you reach, maintain and defend.
