The engine that gets you to fit is the build-measure-learn loop, run with a genuine MVP: a minimum viable product, meaning just enough product to satisfy early customers and generate real feedback. Not your grand vision; the smallest honest test of it. The continent's hardware ventures show the craft. Easy Solar ran a two-hundred-customer pilot before scaling to a million people served. BasiGo started with two imported buses on real Nairobi routes, measured one hundred and fifty thousand kilometres of actual operating data, and only then committed to a local assembly line. Ampersand proved the swap arithmetic with a handful of retrofitted motorcycles and a few Kigali riders before building a network. The loop has five steps: state the hypothesis, build the smallest test, measure real behaviour, not compliments, learn, and repeat. Keep the Module-two discipline: hunt for disproof, not confirmation. And here is the step most climate founders skip: run your impact claims through the same loop. The tonnes of carbon avoided, the incomes raised, the waste diverted: measure them in your pilots, because carbon buyers, development banks and governments will audit every number, and verified impact becomes a revenue stream of its own.
