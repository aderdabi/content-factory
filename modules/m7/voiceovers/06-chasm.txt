Then comes the hardest jump in technology markets: the chasm. Adoption follows a curve: innovators first, about two and a half percent; then early adopters, the visionaries, about thirteen and a half percent; then the early majority, the pragmatists, a full third of the market. And between the visionaries and the pragmatists lies a gap where most ventures die. The mechanism is a credibility paradox: pragmatists only trust references from other pragmatists. But how do you get your first pragmatist reference when no pragmatist has bought yet? You cross deliberately: dominate one niche completely, your beachhead; turn its members into loud references; make the product complete and boring-reliable; and lower every adoption barrier you can find. For climate ventures the stakes are doubled, because systemic impact lives on the far side of the chasm. Eco-enthusiast early adopters are lovely, but the tonnes of carbon are with the pragmatic majority.
