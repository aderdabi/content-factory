And so, your final assignment, the capstone of the course. Write your value hypothesis in one sentence. List your five riskiest remaining assumptions, commercial and impact, each with its smallest MVP experiment, its metric, its threshold and its date. Define your product-market-fit dashboard, including at least one verified impact metric. Build your direct-sales plan with ten named prospects, and choose your distribution play: agents, embedded channels, or aggregators, with the first partner named. Plan your beachhead capture through your persona's real watering holes. And set your expansion bet with a measurable trigger, not a date. That roadmap completes your Venture Workbook: problem, value proposition, market, competition, business model, and now the plan. You came into this course with an idea. You leave with a venture designed to make money by making an impact, and the tools to keep testing it against reality. Now go and build it. Good luck!
